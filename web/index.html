How It Works

Three steps. One window.

No routing, no setup, no plugins to configure. The whole workflow lives in one place.

Drop your drums
Drag a stereo drum recording into the app or use the file picker. WAV, AIFF, FLAC, or MP3. Recorded live, sampled, or programmed — if there are drums in it, it works.
No format conversion  ·  Drag and drop supported
Five stems, separated
Five neural networks run locally and isolate kick, snare, toms, hi-hat, and cymbals into clean individual stems. No cloud. No queues. No uploading your unreleased music to a server.
U-Net architecture  ·  Apple Silicon optimized  ·  Fully offline
Export stems + MIDI
Drag any stem directly from the app into your DAW track. Export MIDI with every hit detected, velocity intact, tempo auto-detected from the drums themselves. Five tracks, ready to use.
Type 1 MIDI  ·  Channel 10  ·  GM drum mapping  ·  Auto BPM

Features

Built for the session.

Every decision was made around one question: what actually slows you down?

Neural stem separation
Five dedicated U-Net models, each trained on a specific drum voice. The kick doesn't bleed into the snare because they're not sharing a model.
Per-stem hit detection
DSP onset detection runs on each separated stem individually. You get accurate timing and velocity per voice — not a guess about where a hit landed.
Drag to DAW
Grab any stem waveform and drag it directly onto a track in Logic, Ableton, Pro Tools, or Reaper. No save-to-folder, no navigate-and-import. Matches how you actually work.
Auto BPM detection
Tempo is estimated directly from your drum hits after separation. The MIDI file exports with the correct BPM baked in — no manual entry, no alignment guesswork in your DAW.
Fully offline
No cloud uploads, no server queues, no API keys, no subscription. Your recordings stay on your drive. Fast because it's local — not because we made you wait.
Standalone + AU + VST3
One download includes the standalone app, an Audio Unit, and a VST3 plugin. Use it outside your DAW for speed, inside for project integration. Same tool, both places.
